About Me

I'm currently a student enrolled in the Master of Physics of Complex System, a master held by Politecnico of Torino, together with SISSA and ICTP in Trieste and various universities in Paris.

I gradueted at the University of Bologna with a bachelor degree in Physics, with a thesis on Deep Learning and Thermodynamics (the link is in the lower section, for the full version you can contact me).

My main interests are Artificial Intelligence, learning algorithms and, more in general, the study of how something can learn something new.

I am actually interested in models spacing also outside of the AI field, particularly when the aim is to build simulation of interesting phenomena related to real world problems.

The link Interesting Stuff contains various simple projects that are mostly basic physical or computational systems that show unusual and amusing properties, or that are just nice to look at.

Together with these nice and fun projects, I try to pursue other (usually bigger) projects. Most of the time I just start something new and after some time either it happens to be less interesting than what I was thinking, or I have other stuff to do (such as study for the university) and I leave them on hold. I am still deciding how to keep track of what I am doing, for now I will probably try to use Twitter (if Elon does not destroy it). My timeline is below (looks very professional), but I don't guarantee I will actually post something.

Anyway, most of these larger projects end up on my Github page, if they ever get finished.
